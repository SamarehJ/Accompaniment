People often ask how this compares to therapy, coaching, or consulting.

Therapy

Therapy focuses on diagnosis, treatment, and your past. Here, we work with what's happening now and what you want next.

Coaching

Coaching typically means domain expertise and ongoing guidance—someone who knows your field and can direct you as long as you're working in it. This work is built differently: I help your own expertise emerge for what you're dealing with, then step away. The relationship is designed to end, not continue.

Consulting

Consulting delivers solutions and takes over your problems. Here, I help your own clarity return.

How It Works

Plan → Act → Reflect

We plan just enough to move. You act in the real world. We reflect on what actually happened. Then we adjust and try again.

Support can include reflection and planning conversations, written exercises, brief check-ins, and concrete suggestions framed as experiments.

The work moves through natural states as you regain traction:

Finding
Ground Building
Momentum Steady
Progress Letting
Go

As you become more capable and self-directed, support deliberately fades. The only required state before ending is Letting Go, where you don't need to rely on me anymore.
